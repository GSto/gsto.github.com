Why I Am Killing My Company Blog

Jul 29th, 2014

Personal vs. Professional Blogging, Part II

Back in March, I wrote and article, Personal vs. Professional Blogging. In that article I explained why I decided to publish my writing on two blogs: glennstovall.com, Where I planned on writing content aimed at fellow developers and freelancers. The other site was Concordant Solutions My “company brand” (ugh, I will never be comfortable with that phrase) where I aimed to write content I thought clients would enjoy. That hasn’t worked. Here is why, and how I plan to fix.

No One Connects With a Company

Unless you are a large company with a lot of authority, Most people aren’t going to be interested in your company blog. KISSMetrics is an exception to this rule. People would rather read an article by “Glenn”, than by “Concordant Solutions”. The second comes across as less authentic.

It’s Easier to Manage One Thing Instead of Two

It’s better to update blogs regularly. By having two blogs maintaining consistency is twice as hard. It also added mental overhead to every article that I wanted to write, since I had to decide where it would go, and what audience I would try to appeal to. Instead, now I can have one content management system, and one stream of content that is more steady. There were even a couple of situations where I tried to spin content so I could post it twice, which was a waste of time.

It Helps SEO (At Least I’m Pretty Sure)

I’m not an SEO Guru. Beyond “Content is king”, “Have a good title tag”, “No one uses the <meta name='keywords'> tag anymore, I know nothing. This is just from anecdotal observation. This domain has been around longer, gotten more traffic, and had more back links. Anything I publish here does better than anything I have published on my company domain. Viral traffic seems to work like high tide coming in: There is a wave of traffic that comes in quickly, and leaves just as fast. But once it is gone, It leaves the average daily visitors mark a little higher than it found it. Google must think this domain has some value. Why squander it?

There is More Crossover Appeal Than I Originally Thought

Most of my clients are small businesses or tech companies, which is not as different of an audience as I thought. Freelancers are by definition also small business owners (even if they don’t see themselves that way). And people at tech companies in a position to hire me often have a technical background. I said that they might not know what AngularJS is, but that is not always the case. I can still write content aimed at a subset of my audience, but there is no reason to have two locations to do so.

At The End of The Day, The Company Blog Was a Failure

I’ve maintained Concordant Solutions for a year, and I am not afraid to admit that as a marketing tool, it has been a failure. I will say that as a sales tool, It has done some good work. The difference is that while that domain and the content on it has not brought me any new leads, it has been a great tool to help turn leads into clients. Potential clients are usually not going to find your portfolio by googling for it; They are going to see your portfolio because they said “can I see your portfolio?” and you mailed them a link.

My company site will still exist for that reason. Any content on there I still deem worthwhile is going to be ported over to this domain over the next couple weeks. The company site will be a resource for past, present, and future clients. I still intend on it being a valuable sales tool. It just won’t have an articles section any longer.
