How to Turn Your Services Into Products

Jul 14th, 2014

Today I wanted to talk to you about the idea of “productized consulting”. Productized can give you a way to package your services in a way that is easier to sell and can be scalable. Here’s a quick run down of how it works:

  • Take a service you have provided to clients in the past.
  • Define a fixed scope and price for that service.
  • Sell that service like you would a physical product or monthly subscription.

WPTheory is a good example. Adam Clark chooses a WordPress theme, install it, customize it, and put up content, in one day. He sells this package for $999. It could be difficult to convince clients to pay you a $1,000 day rate, but by positioning it this way, Adam is able to do this frequently.

While $1,000/day would be nice, what if you could charge $1,000 an hour? That’s what Joanna Weibe did with their consulting product, The no fluff website review. Joanna sends over a 60 minute video about your website, and charges you $997 for the privilege.

Something else that makes Joanna’s example is that while she is still providing a service, she has turned that service into a more tangible deliverable. Instead of just telling the client the changes or writing them a list, she gives them a meeting, a video, and several usable copy writing samples they can use. You can make a lot of services and meetings more valuable to the client by attaching some form of report or usable item as part of it.

I hope this gets the gears turning for you for some new ways to position your services. I thought about writing up a list of more examples of freelancing as a service, but it turns out the guys at Workshop already did it, and they knocked it out of the park.
