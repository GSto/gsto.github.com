Good Copywriters Borrow, Great Copywriters Steal

Sep 29th, 2014 | Comments

I’ve spent a lot of time writing this year, and especially this month with the 30 day publishing challenge. Something I have discovered is that writing is not as hard as it looks. You may think that you have to write for inspiration to strike, and if doesn’t, you can complain about “writer’s block”.

Writer’s block just means you aren’t looking heard enough. There is inspiration for copy everywhere. Pablo Picasso always said “Good artists borrow, great artists steal”. When you don’t know what to say about your company or your product, then it’s time to go on a caper and find some messages you can use in your copy.

Steal From Your Customers

Keep notes on interactions you have with past, present, and future customers. Your copy writing needs to be in your customer’s language, not your own. If you have collected testimonials, that can be a great place to start. Sales meetings, request for proposals, and job board postings are all also good places.

This will also help you create higher converting copy. People like when they read something and think “wow, they really get me!” By speaking your customers language, and addressing points you got straight from the horse’s mouth, you will be more in tune with your customer’s needs.

Steal From Your Competitors

Look at the sites of a few of your competitors, there are three things you want to pay attention to:

  • Their headings and subheadings.
  • Their calls-to-action.
  • Anything else that makes you stop and go “Hmm!”.

You don’t want to take these lines verbatim, but instead get an overall feel for how other people are approaching the market. You can make a spreadsheet of these three things to keep track.

This will also help you make sure that you are saying something unique. You don’t have to be the best in your market, just different. You could even make your headline the opposite of your competitors. If they focus on being the biggest, you can focus on being a smaller, more personal brand, for example.

Steal From Other People’s Feedback

Amazon reviews are another great source for ideas. Search for books related to your services, and see what people are talking about. Look at 5-star reviews to get an idea of what people like, and 1-star reviews to see what problems people have and what you should avoid.

Further Reading

The idea of this approach is to turn writing from a creative practice into a scientific one; From a passive way of writing to an active way. Joanna Weibe of CopyHackers has a great article on this idea: Headline Formulas and the Science of CRO Copy writing
