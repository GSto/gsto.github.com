Be Greeted With Customers, Not Crickets On Launch Day

At the beginning of 2014, I set a new year’s resolution. In fact, I set a lot of them. Some of them stuck, some didn’t, but there was one that was most important to me: Launch a product by the end of the year. I started working on a book, which at the time was entitled Bootstrap Freelance, which was about starting a freelance career from scratch.

There are a lot of eBooks on the internet. New ones are published every day. What could I do to give my book a greater chance of success?

Start Building An Audience.

By building a mailing list of freelancers, I could start building trust and authority in my niche. I would also have an avenue to get feedback from potential customers on my product ideas. Nathan Barry once said “Email is like recurring revenue for marketing.” Every time I wrote a new article, for example, I could send it out to people.

Building an audience gives everything else you do a greater chance of success. You never have to start from zero again. Every piece of content you write will get more reads, and more shares. Any time you release something to your audience, you will get more sales. You will also gain valuable experience in launching a product.

I believe these techniques can work on building any kind of audience. In this guide, You’ll see the processes and tools I used in an absurd amount of detail. Building an audience is extremely valuable, even if you don’t have an idea for a product or service yet.

  • A source of one-on-one conversations and personal relationships.
  • A way to find interested beta testers and get customer feedback.
  • More traffic, more shares, and a greater reach for every new piece of content I write.
  • Never having to start at zero on any project I work on, ever again.
